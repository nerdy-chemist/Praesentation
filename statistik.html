The Great Depression

  • Lasted roughly 1929–1939, affecting the U.S. and the world.
  • Unemployment: Peaked at 25% in the U.S.
  • Homelessness & poverty: Shantytowns called “Hoovervilles” emerged.
  • Bank failures: People lost their life savings; credit dried up.
  • Agriculture: Farmers hit hard; Dust Bowl worsened conditions in the Midwest.
