Stock Market Boom

  • During the late 1920s, the stock market became a symbol of the nation’s prosperity.
  • Millions of Americans invested, believing stock prices would rise forever.
  • Speculation was rampant: investors bought stocks with borrowed money (buying on margin).
  • From 1924 to 1929, the Dow Jones index tripled, creating a bubble of unrealistic confidence.
  • This boom was unsustainable, as stock values were far higher than the companies’ real worth.
