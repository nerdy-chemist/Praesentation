Consumerism and Prosperity

  • Mass production made goods like cars, radios, and household appliances affordable for ordinary families.
  • Henry Ford’s assembly line revolutionized manufacturing, lowering prices and speeding up production.
  • A new culture of buying on credit and installment plans allowed people to purchase items they couldn’t immediately afford, fueling demand but also building debt.
  • Cities grew rapidly as more Americans sought modern lifestyles, leaving behind rural traditions.
  • The 1920s became a symbol of modern consumer culture, where owning the latest products was tied to status and success.
