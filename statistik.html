The Great Depression

  • The Depression lasted from 1929 to the late 1930s, devastating families, businesses, and communities.
  • Unemployment reached 25%, leaving millions without work or income.
  • Thousands of banks failed, wiping out life savings and destroying trust in the financial system.
  • Families lost homes, and makeshift shantytowns, nicknamed “Hoovervilles”, spread in cities.
  • Bank failures: People lost their life savings; credit dried up.
  • Farmers were hit especially hard: falling crop prices and the Dust Bowl droughts in the Midwest forced many to abandon their land.
