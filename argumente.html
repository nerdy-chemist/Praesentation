Black Tuesday (1929)

  • On October 29, 1929, known as Black Tuesday, the stock market collapsed.
  • Panic selling sent stock prices plunging, wiping out fortunes overnight.
  • Banks and investors lost billions of dollars within days.
  • Ordinary people who had invested their savings saw them vanish.
  • This crash was the spark that ignited the Great Depression, the worst economic crisis in U.S. history.
